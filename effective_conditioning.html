Effective Conditioning via Manifold Scaling

This document explains how to change the effective conditioning of the SE(3) optimization problem in Ceres without modifying cost Jacobians.

Short Answer

If your cost functors already return correct Jacobians with respect to the 7D ambient pose

\[ [q_x, q_y, q_z, q_w, t_x, t_y, t_z] \]

then you do not need to modify them.

To change the effective conditioning (what feels like preconditioning in Ceres), you only need to modify:

  1. the SE(3) Plus operation, and
  2. the chart Jacobian / pullback

Ceres always builds the linear system in the local tangent space using these.

What Ceres Does Internally

Let

  • \(x \in \mathbb{R}^7\) be the ambient pose
  • \(\delta \in \mathbb{R}^6\) be the tangent increment
  • \(\text{Plus}(x,\delta)\) be your manifold update
  • \(P(x) = \left.\frac{\partial \text{Plus}(x,\delta)}{\partial \delta}\right|_{\delta=0}\) be the chart Jacobian (7x6)

If your cost functor provides an ambient Jacobian

\[ J_{\text{ambient}} = \frac{\partial r}{\partial x} \in \mathbb{R}^{1 \times 7}, \]

then Ceres forms the local Jacobian as

\[ J_{\text{local}} = J_{\text{ambient}} \, P(x). \]

All Gauss-Newton / LM solves happen in this 6D local space.

The Key Rule

If you change the Plus operation so that it applies a scaled tangent,

\[ \delta' = S \, \delta, \]

then the chart Jacobian must change consistently:

\[ P_{\text{new}}(x) = P_{\text{old}}(x) \, S. \]

Ceres will then automatically build

\[ J_{\text{local}} = J_{\text{ambient}} \, P_{\text{new}}(x), \]

without any change to your cost Jacobians.

Concrete Example: Scaling Rotation vs Translation

Let the tangent be ordered as

\[ \delta = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{v} \\ \boldsymbol{\omega} \end{bmatrix} \in \mathbb{R}^6, \]

with translation \(\mathbf{v}\) and rotation \(\boldsymbol{\omega}\).

Choose a characteristic length \(L\) (same units as translation) and define

\[ S = \begin{bmatrix} I_3 & 0 \\ 0 & L I_3 \end{bmatrix}. \]

This makes "1 radian of rotation" comparable to "\f$L\f$ meters of translation".

Modified Plus Operation

The manifold update becomes

\[ \boldsymbol{\omega}' = L \boldsymbol{\omega}, \quad \mathbf{v}' = \mathbf{v}, \]

\[ R_{\text{new}} = R \exp(\boldsymbol{\omega}'), \]

\[ \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{t} + R \, V(\boldsymbol{\omega}') \, \mathbf{v}'. \]

This is a pure change of variables, not a change of the objective.

Modified Chart Jacobian

At \(\delta = 0\), the SE(3) chart Jacobian has the structure

\[ P(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} & 0 \\ 0 & \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} \end{bmatrix}. \]

After scaling, it becomes

\[ P_{\text{new}}(x) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \, L & 0 \\ 0 & R \end{bmatrix}. \]

Only the rotation columns are scaled. Nothing else changes.

What Remains Unchanged

  • All cost functors
  • All ambient (1x7) Jacobians
  • The quotient-rule correctness of your residual derivatives
  • Your Jacobian pullback code (J7 * P(x))
  • The linear solver (including cuDSS)

You are changing only the metric in which Ceres measures steps on SE(3).

Important Consistency Check

Ceres must be using your manifold implementation (Plus and PlusJacobian) for the 7D pose blocks.

If any default quaternion parameterization is still attached, the pullback will be inconsistent.

Summary

  • Yes, you can keep your Jacobians.
  • Modify Plus and PlusJacobian only.
  • This is the correct and supported way to influence conditioning when using direct solvers in Ceres.
  • Conceptually, this is variable scaling, not a hack.

If you want, the next step is choosing a good value for \(L\) based on your mesh scale or correspondence statistics.
