SE(3) Full Chart Jacobian (Right-Multiplicative)

This document describes the full 7x6 chart Jacobian of the SE(3) Plus operation away from the linearization point \(\delta = 0\). It is provided strictly for reference and mathematical completeness.

For Gauss-Newton optimization, the Jacobian is always evaluated at \(\delta = 0\), which yields the simplified form implemented in SE3.h. The purpose of this document is to explain which terms vanish at the expansion point and why.

This formulation uses the standard matrix Lie algebra parametrization of \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\), obtained by differentiating the orthogonality constraint \(R^\top R = I\), which yields skew-symmetric generators and is equivalent to the axis-angle exponential map used in the implementation.

Conventions

Pose:

\[ x = (q, \mathbf{t}), \qquad q \in S^3,\ \mathbf{t} \in \mathbb{R}^3 \]

Tangent increment (Lie algebra):

\[ \delta = (\mathbf{v}, \boldsymbol{\omega}) \in \mathbb{R}^6 \]

where \(\mathbf{v}\) is translation and \(\boldsymbol{\omega}\) is an axis-angle rotation vector (radians).

Right-multiplicative (body-frame) update:

\[ T_{\text{new}} = T \cdot \exp(\widehat{\delta}) \]

Component form:

\[ \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{q} \otimes \exp(\boldsymbol{\omega}), \qquad \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{t} + R(\mathbf{q}) \, V(\boldsymbol{\omega}) \, \mathbf{v} \]

where \(V(\boldsymbol{\omega})\) is the SO(3) left Jacobian (see SO(3) Left Jacobian).

Definition of the Chart Jacobian

Define the Plus chart:

\[ x^{+}(\delta) = \mathrm{Plus}(x, \delta) \]

The chart Jacobian is:

\[ P(x,\delta) = \frac{\partial\,\mathrm{Plus}(x,\delta)}{\partial \delta} \in \mathbb{R}^{7\times6} \]

Block structure:

\[ P(x,\delta) = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} & \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \\ \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} & \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \end{bmatrix} \]

Blocks That Are Always Zero

Quaternion with respect to translation:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} = 0 \]

Reason: the quaternion update depends only on \(\boldsymbol{\omega}\), not on \(\mathbf{v}\).

Translation w.r.t. Translation

From:

\[ \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{t} + R(\mathbf{q})\,V(\boldsymbol{\omega})\,\mathbf{v} \]

we obtain:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} = R(\mathbf{q})\,V(\boldsymbol{\omega}) \]

At the expansion point \(\boldsymbol{\omega}=0\):

\[ V(0) = I \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \mathbf{v}} = R(\mathbf{q}) \]

Quaternion w.r.t. Rotation

Quaternion update:

\[ \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{q} \otimes \delta\mathbf{q}, \qquad \delta\mathbf{q} = \exp(\boldsymbol{\omega}) \]

Let \(\mathbf{q} = [\mathbf{v}_q, s]\) and \(\delta\mathbf{q} = [\mathbf{v}_\delta, s_\delta]\).

Quaternion multiplication yields:

\[ \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}} = \begin{bmatrix} s\,\mathbf{v}_\delta + s_\delta\,\mathbf{v}_q + \mathbf{v}_q \times \mathbf{v}_\delta \\ s\,s_\delta - \mathbf{v}_q^\top \mathbf{v}_\delta \end{bmatrix} \]

The Jacobian factorizes as:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} = \frac{\partial \mathbf{q}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \delta\mathbf{q}} \frac{\partial \delta\mathbf{q}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \]

At \(\boldsymbol{\omega}=0\):

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{v}_q}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} = \frac{1}{2}\left( s I + [\mathbf{v}_q]_\times \right), \qquad \frac{\partial s}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} = -\frac{1}{2}\mathbf{v}_q^\top \]

Translation w.r.t. Rotation (Away from delta = 0)

From:

\[ \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}} = \mathbf{t} + R(\mathbf{q})\,V(\boldsymbol{\omega})\,\mathbf{v} \]

we obtain:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} = R(\mathbf{q}) \frac{\partial V(\boldsymbol{\omega})}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \mathbf{v} \]

This block is proportional to \(\mathbf{v}\) and is therefore nonzero only away from the linearization point.

At \(\delta=0\) (i.e. \(\mathbf{v}=0\)):

\[ \frac{\partial \mathbf{t}_{\text{new}}}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} = 0 \]

Final Forms

Full chart Jacobian:

\[ P(x,\delta) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & \frac{\partial (\mathbf{q}\otimes\exp(\boldsymbol{\omega}))}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}} \\ R(\mathbf{q})V(\boldsymbol{\omega}) & R(\mathbf{q})\frac{\partial V(\boldsymbol{\omega})}{\partial \boldsymbol{\omega}}\mathbf{v} \end{bmatrix} \]

At the Gauss-Newton expansion point \(\delta=0\):

\[ P(x,0) = \begin{bmatrix} 0 & \frac{1}{2}(sI + [\mathbf{v}_q]_\times) \\ R(\mathbf{q}) & 0 \end{bmatrix} \]

This is the standard SE(3) chart Jacobian used in ICP and Gauss-Newton solvers.
